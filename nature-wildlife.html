WILDLIFE

Morocco has long been a popular tourist destination due to it's diverse in landscapes and cultures and in the midst of great ethnic diversity the country has managed to maintain unity.


The Moroccan landscape is unique and provides four natural landscapes. The climate, the sunshine, the serene coastline, the high plateau the reef, and the Atlas Mountains have a great appeal.

The ancient medinas of the main cities have retained their look and feel over the centuries, with lively markets of colorful spice stalls, leather tanning in the streets, and teahouses.

The dunes of the Sahara, Yoga in isolated beaches, and the peaks of the High Atlas Mountains battle for the interest of tourists on holiday in Morocco, with each possessing impressive, picturesque scenery, and hidden-away traditional villages, filled with crafts, carpets, and a rural way of life.

The itinerary traverse routes of galleries, cafes, shops and bars, led by tour guides who share their diverse knowledge in sociable and thought-provoking tour sessions.


The sand dunes of the Moroccan Sahara create some of the most jaw- dropping views in the world.

Desert and the south of Morocco is a land of mystery, a place where the sun is warm and the people are enigmatic.

Walk over the dunes, camp under the clear skies shining with so many stars,

enjoy the ride of the camel, sand boarding, walk in the so many oasis, learn about the history of the thousand kasbahs, stay with Bedouins ….. And many

more out of the box activities that will make your stay unforgettable.

During your tour, you hear the cry of worship at the rising and setting of the

sun, the gentle winds blow sand and water about in a peaceful manner.
