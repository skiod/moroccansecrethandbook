Morocco is a rich country by its heritage and cultural divers and its gastronomy, which recognized as one of the most refined in the world.

The old city of Fes and Marrakech and the huge south of Morocco hold a fascinating mix of old and new — making it the perfect destination to stock up on traditional arts and crafts as well as modern fashion.


It is the result of a combination of many cultures coming from different horizons: Berber, Arabo-Muslim, Jewish, Andalusian and Mediterranean. Moroccan cuisine owes its success to its ability to blend aromas and spices into unique flavors.


Moroccan meals, drinks, breads and cakes will certainly give you a different taste of the country.

Go Local -Traditional Moroccan cooking with the family:

we encourage you to interact and create a truly unique experience as you spend your half day for lunch or dinner hosted by a Moroccan family, preparing food, visiting the souks and baking at the local farine, finishing with a meal that you’ll all share together in traditional Moroccan style. This class is a complete immersion into the Moroccan culture and family life.


Farm to table cooking:

Our Chef guide you through the process of choosing only the finest ingredients to bring gourmet farm to table cooking into your kitchen. In this tour, we bring you the best hands-on cooking classes and teach you an authentic Moroccan meal of delicious traditional dishes.


souk tasting trails:

you will visit the souks or old medinas where there are varied souks allowing the chance to try traditional Moroccan street food including dried meats, milawi, harsha, briwats, spicy sardines,…. Souks of Honey to taste an array of delicious wild honeys, discuss their flavors and health-giving properties and find out why honey is so important in Moroccan cooking and Islamic culture. We’ll investigate traditional cooking methods by visiting a farnatchi where the water for the communal bath house ‘hammam’ is also heated, and a 400 year old ‘furan’ or communal oven and bakery. Discover the world of spices and their uses and the secrets of the male-oriented domain of the tea den under the guidance of a culinary leader and story-teller.
